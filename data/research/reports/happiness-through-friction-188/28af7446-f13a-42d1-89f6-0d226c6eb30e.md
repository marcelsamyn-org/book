# report.md

Depth-Enabling Prompts, Reflective Thinking, and AI Over-Reliance: Evidence, Measurement, and Practical Guidance

Executive summary
- Depth-enabling prompts (including chain-of-thought (CoT), sketch-of-thought (SoT), tree-of-thought (ToT), and related reasoning-prompts) are designed to surface intermediate reasoning steps, align internal problem decomposition with external information, and foster transparent, auditable reasoning trajectories. Across multiple domains, such prompts have been shown to improve task performance on multi-step reasoning tasks, especially with sufficiently capable models and robust prompting scaffolds (Wei et al., 2022; Kojima et al., 2022; Muennighoff et al., 2025; Zhang et al., 2024) [Wei et al., 2022; Kojima et al., 2022; Muennighoff et al., 2025; Zhang et al., 2024].
- Evidence about reducing “cagey” reliance on AI is mixed. Some lines of work show that enabling explicit reasoning traces can increase transparency and accountability and promote self-critique, while other work shows that internal reasoning traces can be unreliable (faithfulness issues) or exploited by adversaries. Hybrid monitoring frameworks that combine reasoning traces with final-output evaluation tend to offer more robust detection and better management of reliance and risk (Arnav et al., 2025; Lanham et al., 2022; Arcuschin et al., 2025) [Arnav et al., 2025; Lanham et al., 2022; Arcuschin et al., 2025].
- Output-quality measurement for depth-enabled prompts should rely on multi-metric evaluation that includes: (a) task performance (accuracy, robustness across domains), (b) reasoning trace quality (faithfulness, coherence, relevance to final outputs), (c) cognitive-load proxies (latency, token usage), (d) transfer/generalization (domain transfer), and (e) safety/robustness (hallucination risk, bias). Several bodies of evidence and practice point to using a combination of automated metrics, human judgments, and model-based evaluators (G-Eval, GPTScore, SelfCheckGPT) to triangulate performance (Lee et al., 2025; Helal et al., 2025; Wang et al., 2025; Ali et al., 2024) [Lee et al., 2025; Helal et al., 2025; Wang et al., 2025; Ali et al., 2024].
- Actionable guidance: adopt a structured measurement framework that explicitly captures reflective-thinking proxies, relies on robust multi-metric evaluation (including uncertainty/signaling and faithfulness), and includes transfer tests across domains. Use domain-adapted prompts and governance approaches to maintain safety and accountability when deploying depth-enabled prompts in high-stakes contexts (KAG-Thinker; Helal et al.; Arnav et al.; Khojasteh et al., 2025; 2025 CHI).

1) Background and motivation
- What depth-enabling prompts target
  - Depth-enabling prompts are designed to elicit internal planning, multi-step reasoning, and explicit justification in AI outputs, with the aim of increasing reflective thinking and reducing superficial, cue-based reliance on AI (CoT, ToT, SoT, ReAct variants) [Wei et al., 2022; Kojima et al., 2022; Zhang et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2025].
- Why measurement matters
  - Prior work shows that simply surfacing intermediate steps can improve task performance on multi-step problems but may also introduce new risks (hallucinations, unfaithful reasoning). Therefore, a robust measurement framework is needed to capture reflective thinking, cognitive load, transfer, and output quality (Lee et al., 2025; Arnav et al., 2025; Lanham et al., 2022; Wu et al., 2024) [Lee et al., 2025; Arnav et al., 2025; Lanham et al., 2022].
- Domains and evidence bases
  - Broad evidence base across education, medicine, software engineering, and multi-domain QA, including large-scale prompting studies (KD-CoT, KiRAG, CoT-RAG, SurgRAW, CoTDet, Web-CogReasoner) and domain-specific demonstrations (KAG frameworks; Khojasteh et al., 2025; Ali et al., 2024; Helal et al., 2025) [KD-CoT; KiRAG; CoT-RAG; SurgRAW; CoTDet; Web-CogReasoner; KAG; Khojasteh et al.; Ali et al.; Helal et al.].

2) Core concepts and theoretical foundations
- Chain-of-Thought prompting (CoT)
  - CoT prompts elicit intermediate steps before final answers, improving performance on arithmetic, commonsense, symbolic reasoning, and multi-hop QA; gains are model-size dependent and often scale with emergent reasoning capabilities (Wei et al., 2022; Kojima et al., 2022; Muennighoff et al., 2025) [Wei 2022; Kojima 2022; Muennighoff 2025].
- SoT, ToT, and related structures
  - SoT yields shorter reasoning sketches with substantial token-cost savings; ToT enables backtracking and search in reasoning paths for reliability on complex tasks. These frameworks support a structured approach to depth-enabled reasoning (SoT; ToT) [Ma et al., respective references in CoT literature].
- Data and prompts for reasoning
  - Step-by-step annotated data and reasoning traces are essential for training and evaluating models’ reasoning and for measuring depth and transfer (Muennighoff et al., 2025; Lanham et al., 2022) [Muennighoff 2025; Lanham 2022].
- Measurement frameworks
  - Multimodal CoT variants, auto-CoT, and active prompting approaches show that prompt engineering can scale reasoning demonstrations; evaluation frameworks increasingly include human judgments and model-based evaluators (Lee et al., 2025; Zhang et al., 2024; Ali et al., 2024) [Lee 2025; Zhang 2024; Ali 2024].
- Safety, faithfulness, and governance
  - Faithfulness (trace accuracy) and hallucinatory risk remain central concerns; hybrid models and verification loops (coherence checks, external retrieval grounding) reduce risk and improve reliability (Lanham 2022; Arnav 2025; CoV-RAG; CoVe) [Lanham 2022; Arnav 2025; He 2024; Ngassom 2024].

3) Evidence for depth-enabled prompts increasing reflective thinking
- KAG-Thinker (Knowledge-Augmented Generation Thinker)
  - Depth solving and logical-form propagation support deeper analysis and robust transfer across steps; autonomous decisions about retrieval reduce unnecessary external queries, improving robustness to noise and enabling structured reasoning with explicit dependencies (KAG-Thinker, 2025) [Sections describing Logical Form; Depth Solving; Knowledge Boundary Determination].
  - Empirical claims: average improvement of 4.1% on seven benchmark datasets; KAG-Med-Thinker (14B) domain-specific medical QA; deep reasoning trajectories and explicit tracing are core artifacts (KAG-Thinker, 2025) [Abstract; 4; 5].
- CoT evidence across benchmarks
  - Zero-shot CoT: introducing “Let’s think step by step” improves performance on several tasks; CoT can be complemented by item-level context and rubrics to boost accuracy (Lee et al., 2024) [Lee et al., 2024].
  - Self-consistency and CoT variants yield persistent improvements on GSM8K, SVAMP, AQuA, etc., especially when multiple reasoning chains are sampled and aggregated (Wang et al., 2022; Chen et al., 2025; Zhang et al., 2024) [Wei 2022; Wang 2022; Chen 2025; Zhang 2024].
- Education and metacognition
  - In educational contexts, depth prompts plus metacognitive training improved self-regulated learning (SRL) and learning experience; however, short-term achievement gains were not guaranteed, and AI reliance risk remains if metacognitive scaffolding is absent (Xu et al., 2025) [Xu et al., 2025].
- Safety, auditability, and governance
  - R2A2-style frameworks argue that prompts should provide explicit audit logs and explanations for decisions, with an emphasis on governance and safety; CoT traces are valuable when coupled with robust auditing and verification (Yan et al., 2025; Raza et al., 2025) [Yan et al.; Raza et al.].
- Domain-specific evidence
  - Medical domain adaptation (MedPaLM-like improvements with instruction tuning; LLaMA-Clinic) demonstrates that domain-adaptive prompting and grounding can improve reliability and safety in high-stakes settings; CoT and reasoning prompts align with these strategies (Wang et al., 2025; Ali et al., 2024) [Wang 2025; Ali 2024].

4) Evidence synthesis: Do depth-enabling prompts increase reflective thinking?
- Overall pattern
  - There is converging evidence that prompts encouraging intermediate reasoning steps can increase the visibility and traceability of cognitive processes, support error detection, and improve final outputs in many domains, particularly with larger models and robust prompting templates (Wei 2022; Kojima 2022; Muennighoff 2025; Zhang 2024; KD-CoT, KiRAG, CoT-RAG, SurgRAW, CoTDet; Web-CogReasoner) [Wei 2022; Kojima 2022; Muennighoff 2025; Zhang 2024; KD-CoT; KiRAG; CoT-RAG; SurgRAW; CoTDet; Web-CogReasoner].
- Reduction of AI dependence (cagey behavior)
  - Mixed evidence: some studies show growing transparency and self-critique with CoT-based prompts, while others show that faithful traces can be manipulated or misrepresent actual reasoning. Hybrid monitoring (reasoning trace + final output) increases robustness and reduces misalignment risk (Arnav et al., 2025; Lanham 2022).
- Output quality measurement implications
  - A multi-metric approach is essential; rely on task performance, reasoning trace fidelity, and safety metrics; where possible, use LLM-based evaluators (G-Eval, GPTScore, SelfCheckGPT) in combination with human judgments to gauge interpretability and trust (Lee et al., 2025; Helal et al., 2025; Wang et al., 2025).

5) Measurement frameworks: practical guidance and a proposed protocol
- A multi-layer measurement framework
  - Reflective thinking proxies
    - Presence, depth, and coherence of reasoning traces; explicit self-checks and self-critique; alignment between steps and final conclusions.
  - Cognitive load proxies
    - Latency, token counts, and, where feasible, subjective workload measures (NASA-TLX); in-language or physiological proxies if practical.
  - Output quality
    - Task accuracy, factuality, coherence, relevance, and alignment with sources; bias and safety checks; fidelity to evidence used.
  - Transfer/generalization
    - Cross-domain performance; long-horizon transfer tasks; cross-modal applicability.
  - AI-dependence indicators
    - Degree of reliance on AI steps vs. user-generated steps; rate of automated or automated-assisted errors; resilience to prompt perturbations.
- Implementation blueprint
  - Phase 1: Baseline prompting (no depth prompts) vs Phase 2: Depth-enabling prompting (CoT/ToT/SoT variants) in multiple domains.
  - Phase 2 includes an uncertainty/signal layer (epistemic/aleatoric) and a verification layer (external retrieval grounding or verifier models) to calibrate outputs and assess trace faithfulness.
  - Phase 3: Transfer and robustness tasks; Phase 4: Longevity study (persistence after AI assistance is reduced or removed).
- Metrics and tools
  - Automated metrics: standard NLP metrics (BLEU, ROUGE, METEOR) with caveats; perplexity; domain-specific accuracy.
  - Human evaluation: rubric-based judgments on reasoning depth, trace fidelity, and support for final conclusions; expert panels when possible.
  - Model-based evaluators: GPTScore, SelfCheckGPT, Prometheus, G-Eval to triangulate judgments.
  - Uncertainty metrics: calibration curves for predictive vs. actual outcomes; entropy-based measures for predictive and semantic uncertainty.
  - Safety and governance: hallucinatory risk indicators; bias/fairness metrics; privacy safeguards; auditability indicators.

6) Domain considerations and caveats
- Many depth-prompt studies are domain-agnostic or domain-agnostic scaffolds; domain-specific demonstrations (medicine, engineering, law) require domain-relevant data, task designs, and evaluation rubrics.
- Faithfulness of CoT traces is an ongoing challenge; ensuring robust auditing and verification (e.g., CoV-RAG, verification loops) is essential for trustworthy deployment in high-stakes contexts.
- Model-scale dependency: many depth-prompt benefits scale with model capacity; smaller models may not realize the same gains; scale-specific evaluation is essential (Muennighoff 2025; Krathwohl 2002—taxonomy framing; Koichiro et al. 2024).

7) Actionable guidance for authors and researchers
- Use a hierarchical prompting approach (Level-2 CoT; Level-4 Least-to-Most; Level-5 Generated Knowledge, etc.) matched to task complexity (Budagam et al., 2025; Helal et al., 2025).
- Pair depth prompts with external grounding and verification loops, including RAG, evidence-based prompts, and domain-specific knowledge graphs to improve faithfulness and reduce hallucinations (KD-CoT; CoV-RAG; KiRAG; Web-CogReasoner; KD-CoT).
- Establish a multi-metric evaluation plan that aggregates judgment across multiple rubrics and evaluators (LLM-as-a-judge; human evaluators) to stabilize inference across tasks and model families (Lee et al., 2025; Ali et al., 2024).
- Design domain-adaptive governance structures (R2A2; CaTE guidebook; DI-GAI-CT) to ensure that depth-enabling prompts do not undermine safety, privacy, or accountability (Yan et al.; Raza et al.; Atf et al.; Ali et al.).
- Integrate cognitive-load and transfer tests into longitudinal studies to capture the durability of reflective-thinking gains and the generalization of reasoning across domains (Xu et al., 2025; Helal et al., 2025; Ali et al., 2024).
- Report both improvement in output quality and any observed costs (latency, computation, cognitive load) to allow practitioners to weigh trade-offs for their domain-specific needs.

8) Limitations of the evidence base
- A substantial portion of the literature provides evidence of improved reasoning quality and more transparent prompts, but often without consistent quantitative effect sizes across domains; many studies emphasize qualitative insights or model-agnostic architectures. There is a need for standardized, domain-specific, longitudinal, multi-metric studies with explicit metrics for reflective thinking and AI reliance (Zhai et al., 2024; Helal et al., 2025; Lee et al., 2025; KD-CoT family; Khojasteh et al., 2025). 

9) Concluding remarks
- Depth-enabling prompts have demonstrated substantial promise to surface internal reasoning, improve reasoning quality, and increase transparency in many contexts, particularly with larger models and well-structured prompting templates. However, capturing reflective thinking and reducing cagey AI dependence require robust, multi-stream evaluation protocols that integrate reasoning traces with outcome metrics, uncertainty signaling, and domain-specific safety considerations. Domain-adaptive governance, cross-domain transfer testing, and explicit measurement of cognitive load and transfer are critical to determining when depth-enabling prompts provide net benefit. The evidence base supports a measured approach to deploying depth-enabling prompts, paired with rigorous evaluation, domain adaptation, and governance.
  
Actionable insights for authors and practitioners (summary)
- Design depth-enabling prompts that explicitly elicit reasoning steps, justification, uncertainty signals, and cross-checks with external sources. Pair prompts with explicit verification loops and external grounding.
- Use a multi-metric evaluation framework that jointly analyzes reflective thinking proxies (trace depth, justification quality, self-critique), cognitive-load proxies (latency, tokens, subjective effort), and output-quality measures (domain-appropriate accuracy, coherence, factuality, and alignment with sources). Include transfer tests to assess generalization.
- Consider hybrid monitoring approaches (reasoning trace + final output) to strengthen detection of over-reliance and to improve robustness to deception or manipulation of traces.
- In high-stakes domains, implement governance and safety layers (verification, auditing, privacy protection) alongside depth-promoting prompts, ensuring transparency and accountability.
- Plan domain-adaptive evaluation programs that test cross-domain generalization and transfer, while controlling for model scale effects and domain-specific data constraints.
