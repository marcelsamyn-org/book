# report.md

Does presenting diversified AI outputs in group discussions boost creativity and reduce convergence bias, compared with single-output prompts?

Executive summary
- The evidence base offers mixed but converging signals. Diversified AI outputs can broaden the group exploration space and accelerate the emergence of diversity in ideas, suggesting potential reductions in convergence bias at the group level. However, diversified AI outputs do not universally elevate individual creativity, and in some studies, AI prompts can drive convergence or homogenization if not carefully designed (Ashkinaze et al., 2025; Doshi & Hauser, 2024; Meincke et al., 2025; Wan & Kalman, 2025; Baltà-Salvador et al., 2026).
- Direct, controlled experiments explicitly comparing diversified multi-output prompts against single-output prompts in live group discussions remain relatively rare. The strongest direct evidence for group-level diversification effects comes from large, dynamic experiments showing higher AI-output exposure can increase collective diversity, sometimes without boosting individual creativity (Ashkinaze et al., 2025). Emerging work on diversified AI prompts (e.g., multiple personas) has shown potential to increase inter-output diversity, though not always surpassing human-generated diversity (Wan & Kalman, 2025).
- Practical takeaway: if the goal is to counter convergence bias in group ideation, deploying diversified AI outputs (multi-output prompts, multi-modal or multi-perspective stimuli) and structuring group discussion to preserve divergent exploration while retaining human judgment appears promising. It should be paired with deliberate evaluation, human-in-the-loop checks, and monitoring of both group diversity and individual creativity (Ashkinaze et al., 2025; Doshi & Hauser, 2024; Baltà-Salvador et al., 2026).

1) Background and definitional frame
- Convergence bias in group ideation: a tendency for groups to narrow from a broad set of ideas to a smaller, more similar subset, potentially suppressing novelty (Seeber et al., 2018; general convergence literature). In AI-assisted contexts, this can be amplified if participants gravitate toward a small number of AI-provided ideas or prompts.
- Diversified AI outputs: an intervention that presents multiple AI-generated ideas, prompts, or stimuli (across models, prompts, modalities, or personas) rather than a single AI output per prompt. The hypothesis is that diversification broadens the initial search space, sustaining exploratory talk and reducing premature convergence (Ashkinaze et al., 2025; Wan & Kalman, 2025; Meincke et al., 2024/2025).

2) What the existing evidence says about AI outputs, creativity, and convergence

2.1 Large dynamic experiment on AI ideas (Ashkinaze et al., CI 2025)
- Design and intervention: Participants (N > 800, 40+ countries) viewed AI-generated ideas or human ideas, with AI-output exposure varied (none, low, high) and stimuli dynamically propagated through randomization and response chains. Outcome measures included Creativity (local/global) and Idea Diversity (local/global/evolution) (Ashkinaze et al., 2025).
- Core findings:
  - High AI exposure increases collective diversity and accelerates its development over time, relative to low or no AI exposure (Ashkinaze et al., 2025).
  - Merely seeing AI ideas does not increase participants’ own creativity; creativity effects were not enhanced by higher AI exposure in terms of individual originality (Ashkinaze et al., 2025).
  - AI-disclosure effects are minimal overall, with moderation by task difficulty and self-identified creativity for AI idea adoption (Ashkinaze et al., 2025).
- Interpretation: Diversified AI inputs can broaden the group’s idea space and speed diversification dynamics, suggesting a reduction in convergence bias at the group level. However, individuals may not experience enhanced creativity from exposure alone, and the effect of disclosing AI-origin ideas is nuanced (Ashkinaze et al., 2025).

2.2 AI reduces group diversity under single-output prompts (Doshi & Hauser, 2024)
- Design: Online, three-arm experiment with short fiction tasks: human-only, human with one AI idea, human with up to five AI ideas (AI-generated ideas via GPT-4). Evaluators rated novelty and usefulness; authors also captured perceived creativity.
- Core findings:
  - AI ideas boost perceived creativity, writing quality, and enjoyment, especially for less creative writers (Doshi & Hauser, 2024).
  - AI-enabled outputs are more similar to each other than human-generated outputs, indicating reduced collective diversity/convergence toward AI-derived content (Doshi & Hauser, 2024).
- Interpretation: There is a documented trade-off: AI can enhance individual creativity but tends to homogenize the group output unless diversity-promoting design features are introduced. This underscores the risk that diversification alone (without explicit diversity-promoting prompts) may not suffice to sustain group-level novelty.

2.3 Mixed findings on diversification with diversified prompts (Meincke et al., 2024–2025)
- Core message: Prompt engineering can boost AI-output diversity, with Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting yielding the highest diversity among tested prompts, approaching human-group dispersion in some contexts (Meincke, Mollick, Terwiesch; SSRN 2024; Nat. Hum. Behav. 2025).
- Implications: Diversified prompts can push AI outputs toward greater dispersion, suggesting a viable route to counter convergence bias in group ideation. Yet AI outputs often remain less diverse than human outputs in some domains and contexts, meaning diversification strategies may need to combine AI prompts with human-guided exploration (Meincke et al., 2024; Wan & Kalman, 2025).

2.4 Collective diversity through diversified AI personas (Wan & Kalman, 2025)
- Design: Ten diverse GenAI personas generated 300 plot ideas; humans wrote stories based on these prompts. Intra-persona ideas were highly similar, but inter-persona diversity was substantial; human-generated stories preserved or matched diversity; AI-generated outputs were more language-diverse.
- Findings: Introducing diverse AI personas can preserve and even enhance collective diversity when humans produce outputs based on diverse AI prompts. This provides a promising design lever for group ideation to counter convergence bias.
- Limitation: This work involves non-real-time group brainstorming in a writing context; generalization to live group discussions requires direct testing (Wan & Kalman, 2025).

2.5 Multi-agent and collaboration-focused perspectives (Chen et al., 2025; Baltà-Salvadór et al., 2026; HO-level work)
- Chen et al. (arXiv, 2025) and related multi-agent collaboration work suggest that diversified AI inputs and leader-facilitated coordination can improve the quality of collaborative ideas, with cognitive diversity as a driver and leadership as a critical moderator. This provides theoretical support for diversified outputs in group settings.
- Baltà-Salvadór et al. (2026) show that AI can preserve thematic diversity while driving semantic convergence and reducing creativity in some contexts; early AI exposure can yield lasting originality benefits across tasks. This highlights the importance of task design and temporal structure when using AI prompts in groups.

2.6 Complementary perspectives on group dynamics and design (Seeber et al., 2018; Vibe-teaming; AI in design)
- Convergence in teams can be mitigated by attention-guiding and discussion-encouragement interventions in controlled experiments, with convergence quality and satisfaction increasing after convergence (Seeber et al., 2018). While not AI-specific, these findings illuminate how explicit structuring of group processes can influence convergence, a consideration for AI-assisted diversified prompts.

3) Synthesis of patterns relevant to your question

- Diversified AI outputs and group diversity:
  - Strongest direct signal for reducing convergence bias at the group level comes from Ashkinaze et al. (2025): high exposure to AI-generated ideas increases collective diversity and speeds its emergence.
  - Wan & Kalman (2025) indicate that using diverse AI personas can sustain inter-persona diversity in human outputs, supporting diversification as a viable mechanism to counter convergence bias at the group level.

- Creativity at the individual vs group level:
  - Across multiple studies, AI outputs tend to boost perceived or measured individual creativity in some contexts (e.g., Doshi & Hauser, 2024; Chak & colleagues; Baltà-Salvador et al., 2026), but there are notable contexts where individual creativity improves while group diversity declines (Doshi & Hauser, 2024). Baltà-Salvador et al. (2026) show nuanced results with semantic convergence despite preserved or enhanced diversity in other dimensions.

- The role of disclosure and moderating factors:
  - AI disclosure generally has limited main effects, but moderation by task difficulty and participant creativity identity appears in Ashkinaze et al. (2025). This implies that how AI is presented (disclosed or not) and who is using it matters for adoption and effects on creativity/diversity.

- Mechanisms for implementing diversification:
  - Multi-output prompts (diversified prompts, multiple AI outputs per prompt) appear to be a central lever for increasing diversity; strategies include prompt chaining, analogical prompting, persona prompting, and retrieval-augmented generation (Meincke et al., 2024; 2025; Wan & Kalman, 2025).

4) Implications for practice and research design

- If your goal is to counter convergence bias in group brainstorming:
  - Deploy diversified AI outputs rather than a single AI prompt per prompt cycle (e.g., provide 3–5 varied ideas or prompts per turn, possibly from different personas or models).
  - Use structured divergence-first workflows: present diverse AI outputs to seed exploration, followed by a facilitated convergent phase that evaluates and synthesizes across divergent threads.
  - Consider cross-modal diversification (text, image, 3D) when the task allows; evidence suggests modality diversity can broaden ideation space and support different kinds of creative reasoning (Wang et al., 2025; Peng et al., 2024; Baltà-Salvadór et al., 2026).
  - Pair AI outputs with explicit human evaluation and scaffolds to safeguard novelty with practicality.

- If your goal is to maximize individual creativity while maintaining group diversity:
  - Provide AI prompts with diversification to boost individual creativity (especially for lower-baseline creativity participants) while ensuring group workflows maintain diversity with explicit prompts to explore alternate directions and cross-domain ideas (Doshi & Hauser, 2024; Ashkinaze et al., 2025).

- Experimental design recommendations to establish causal effects
  - Randomized controlled trials comparing:
    - Condition A: single-output AI prompts per prompt (baseline).
    - Condition B: diversified AI outputs (3–5 outputs per prompt) across multiple prompts/personas or modalities.
  - Measures:
    - Individual creativity: standardized originality/novelty and usefulness ratings.
    - Group diversity: semantic diversity (embedding-based), topic diversity (concept categories), and convergence metrics (e.g., rate of drop in idea variety).
    - Adoption dynamics: AI adoption rates, influenced by task difficulty and disclosure.
  - Analytic approach: mixed-effects models to account for group-level random effects; preregistration of hypotheses; report effect sizes and confidence intervals.

- Ethical and governance considerations
  - Maintain human-in-the-loop evaluation and governance to avoid over-reliance on AI.
  - Transparently disclose AI involvement and ensure accountability for final ideas.
  - Monitor for biases in AI outputs, ensure diverse representation in prompts (e.g., personas), and guard against homogenization due to model priors.

5) Actionable, concrete recommendations for authors and practitioners

- For researchers planning experiments:
  - Design a direct, controlled study to compare diversified-output prompts (multi-output AI prompts, multiple personas, or multi-modal outputs) against single-output prompts in live group brainstorming.
  - Use a standardized, preregistered protocol with valid creativity and convergence metrics, including both qualitative and quantitative measures.
  - Examine moderators: participant creativity identity, task difficulty, group size, and modality (text vs image vs 3D).

- For practitioners implementing AI-assisted group ideation:
  - Introduce diversified AI outputs as a standard practice to seed divergent thinking, but structure the workflow to prevent premature convergence (e.g., separate divergent and convergent phases; encourage parallel exploration across idea streams).
  - Leverage multi-modal AI prompts (text, images, prototypes) to broaden ideation channels and keep exploration rich.
  - Incorporate human-in-the-loop checks and explicit evaluation rubrics that balance novelty with usefulness, feasibility, and domain relevance.
  - Monitor and adapt the level of AI disclosure based on task difficulty and group composition; be mindful of moderation effects by participant creativity identity.

- For educators and designers:
  - When teaching or designing group tasks with AI, emphasize the role of diversified prompts and multiple AI sources to maximize the diversity of outcomes.
  - Introduce prompts that explicitly call for contrasting or divergent ideas and for recombining ideas across domains.

6) Limitations and caveats
- Direct, controlled evidence comparing diversified multi-output prompts to single-output prompts in live group discussions is still limited. The strongest results come from large-scale studies showing increased group diversity with higher exposure to AI ideas, but not universal boosts in individual creativity (Ashkinaze et al., 2025; Doshi & Hauser, 2024; Meincke et al., 2024/2025; Wan & Kalman, 2025; Baltà-Salvadór et al., 2026).
- Task domains vary across studies (writing, design, product ideation, scenario planning). Generalizability requires replication across contexts and task types.
- The presence of AI can change group dynamics in complex ways; careful design, governance, and ongoing evaluation are essential.

7) Conclusion
- There is credible, converging evidence that presenting diversified AI outputs can boost group-level diversity and accelerate the emergence of diverse ideas, which is a key lever against convergence bias in group ideation. However, evidence about whether diversification reliably increases individual creativity is mixed and context-dependent. The literature strongly suggests that diversification strategies—especially multi-output prompts, diverse personas, cross-modal outputs, and MI-CC-style facilitation—hold promise for more creative and diverse group outcomes, but require careful design, explicit measurement, and robust empirical testing in the target domain.
